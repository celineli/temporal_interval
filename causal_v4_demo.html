New control conditions
Condition 4
Scrambled-face physical
Same mechanic as Cond 1 (slides away) — patient has face PARTS, but jumbled / non-face-like arrangement
Condition 5
Mouth-only mental
Same mechanic as Cond 2 (stays still, expression changes) — only a large, salient mouth, no eyes/nose
Condition 6
Explosion → agent surprise
Block A hits Block B → Block B explodes → Agent C (eyes toward the explosion, standing apart, no contact) changes from happy to surprised
Condition 7
Sleep → awake (eyes open toward cause)
Same mechanic as Cond 5 — blue ball approaches, yellow ball stays still. Eyes change from closed (asleep) to open, gaze directed toward the blue ball. Nose and neutral mouth throughout.
Condition 8
Neutral → angry
Agent starts neutral (open eyes toward ball, flat mouth). After contact: eyes squint with inner brows pulled down, mouth becomes a frown arc.
Condition 9
Neutral → pain
Agent starts neutral. After contact: eyes become >< (screwed shut in pain), mouth becomes an inverted triangle / pyramid shape.

Logic: if temporal contiguity tolerance follows Cond 1 (physical launch) > Cond 3 (color change) > Cond 2 (emotional change), these conditions test why.   Cond 4 asks whether face parts without a coherent gestalt change delay tolerance relative to Cond 1.   Cond 5 asks whether mouth alone reproduces Cond 2 mental-effect tolerance.   Cond 6 separates contact from cause via explosion.   Cond 7 tests attentional state change (sleep→awake) without emotional valence.   Cond 8 tests negative-valence emotional response (anger) — does interpersonal/aggressive mental state show different PSE from sadness (Cond 2)?   Cond 9 tests physical-pain expression — situates pain between pure physical effect (Cond 1) and emotional/social effect (Cond 2) to probe whether embodied pain reads more like physical or mental causation.
